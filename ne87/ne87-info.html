~ ~ ~ Welcome to NE87: Backup6! ~ ~ ~

Hosted by @Lagavulin, with co-hosts @Stuck(Love Of Aqua’s Life) and @queenhails, this balanced semi-open 9p (7 towns vs 2 mafias) setup is a variation of Matrix6 designed to be more balanced then the original. Six roles are always the same and the remaining 3 are determined by random rolling against a matrix. You can read about the design theory here.

Setup (9p)

+ Citizen + Citizen + Citizen + Citizen + Citizen + Random Town + Random Town - Goon - Random Mafia To determine the setup, a random number from 1 to 3 or a random letter from A to C is drawn. After that the corresponding column or row of that letter or number is selected as the game's setup matrix below. E.g.: The letter B is randomly chosen, the setup will now consist of a Cop, 6 Citizen. and 2 Goons. The setup is not revealed to the players after that, so the players themselves are unaware of which setup, out of the 6 available ones, they are actually playing:

Lunexia 2025-01-20 2:48 a.m.
Hey everyone, I am SO PROUD of how everyone played, you guys did so so well omg!!! WHAT AN EXCITING FINAL 3 YOU GUYS ROCKKKKKKKKKKKKK! I wanted to quickly discuss Final 3, and what a very standard practice is for it. Crossing! To cross means to vote each other, and crossing most often refers to doing that while in lylo of some sort (final 5 with 2 mafia, final 3 with 1 mafia, etc). Usually, what you do on these days, is have the two scummiest players vote each other, this is usually the most optimal player. You discuss who the least suspicious player is, to reduce the odds of town being voted and then who the most suspicious are to increase the odds of mafia being voted, then these two are the first to vote, and they vote each other. This will almost guarantee a 60-70% chance to vote mafia, unless the mafia played insanely well, of course. (or the town really poorly) So far above just RNGing it! You guys took a long while to do this, but got there in the end ^^ Crossing can honestly be done rather early into lylo days though! Since then, the people who are not crossing will have a LOT more time to discuss between just two final choices for that day, and then make their best judgement off of that. Which is by far the hardest part of a lylo day. Making the correct choice in the cross. This is the time where the two players in the cross give their final defenses and try to refute the claims made against them, while the ones who are not crossing are to decide who they trust and make their final arguments. This can repeat on future lylo days, as then you will have even more information! Make sure to keep an open mind during consecutive lylos, but of course, dont question your solves too much either I think you guys played this really well, good job!
