AR6 - Secret Hitler The year is 1932, welcome to pre-WWII Germany! You are a German politician trying your best to keep the Liberal government intact and to quell the rising groups of fascists seeking to implement their own beliefs into the government. Game Summary Secret Hitler is begun with players being sorted into three roles: Liberals, Fascists, and Hitler. The Liberals always have majority, but unlike the Fascists who are always aware of eachother's identities, are uninformed and must try their hardest to deduce who can truly be trusted. There are two phases: The Chancellor nomination and the Legislative session. During the Chancellor nomination, a random player is chosen to be the President. The President chooses another player to become the Chancellor, and everyone may vote with "Ja!" or "Nein!" to decide if the Chancellor is elected or not. If the vote succeeds, the game moves to the Legislative session. The president is then given three random policies and must choose one to discard. These last two policies are then given to the Chancellor, who must choose one of the two to enact. The cycle then repeats. If the Chancellor vote does not succeed, then a tack is added to the Nomination counter. If the Chancellor nomination fails three times, anarchy will occur. A Fascist policy will instantly be enacted. As more Fascist policies are enacted, Presidental Powers may come into play. See the full rules for details. Liberals win once five Liberal policies have been enacted or if Hitler is chosen for execution. Fascists win once six Fascist policies have been enacted, or if Hitler is elected Chancellor after three Fascist policies have been enacted. More details can be found here.
