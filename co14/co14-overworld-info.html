Welcome to ~ ~ ~ Hyperdimensional Village Tohuwabohu! ~ ~ ~, a Minecraft story!

This calm village has been generated by the players a good while back, however, it's been forgotten and left unrendered for most of the time. Until one day, suddenly, it got automatically rendered? No player around? What's going on? Turns out, the old End Portal underneath the village has been activated! But with rendered chunks, come unwelcome guests, and so, it has been observed that villagers go missing every night.. It must be the monsters! Who else would dare disrupt the peace of the village! Find them, and throw them into the end portal, before its too late!

This is a ~ ~ ~ Semi-Closed ~ ~ ~ setup!

That means, that parts of the setup are known, while other parts of the setup are for you to explore and figure out while playing! Be prepared for some wonderful, or wicked, surprises!

Role Distribution

- Base Roles - Each alignment has certain base roles, they outline the wincons among other things. - Professions - Each player, regardless of alignment and base role, will be given a profession to play as. Professions are given out as described below. - Profession Draft - Before the game starts, there will be a 24h drafting phase. At the start of the draft, all players receive their base roles. Then, all players are presented with - 3 professions - and may choose 1 to keep! The profession chosen will then grant the player their profession role! One special role per player! Special roles are as follows - King - Mayor - Sheriff All special roles have a: - 60% chance to roll for a villager. - 20% chance to roll for a monster. - 20% chance to roll for a chaotic creature. Professions

The following professions exist: - Armorer - Butcher - Cartographer - Cleric - Farmer - Fisherman - Fletcher - Leatherworker - Librarian - Mason - Shepherd - Toolsmith - Weaponsmith

Special Roles

The King Kings Decree: Your votepower is permanently set to 3 and you are revealed as the King at the start of the game. Kings Lineage: At night, choose a player, they will become your descendant at the end of the night on a successful visit. Your descendant will receive the King role should you die. You may change who your descendant is again during future nights. Who your descendant is will not be revealed to anyone until you die. - This will not occur should a day start with 9 or less players. The Mayor Mayors Influence: Your votepower is permanently set to 2 and you are revealed as the Mayor at the start of the game. Additionally, if there is a tie, and you vote one of the tied players, the player you voted will die. Mayor Election: Upon death, a private vote is held during the first 24 hours of the next day phase, only the top vote is revealed, not who voted who, on a tie, an unannounced RNG tiebreaker happens. - This will not occur should a day start with 9 or less players. The Sheriff Deputy Appointment: Appoint a Deputy during the night, this is a bonus action. The deputy will learn your name and the two of you will open a chat together. This action becomes unusable as soon as it succeeds. If the appointed player dies that night, this action fails. Lost Mentor: If you die, then the Deputy may no longer act. The Deputy Deputy Appointment: This special role must be appointed by the Sheriff. Once appointed you receive this role at the end of the night, receive a chat with the Sheriff, and become revealed as the Deputy at the start of the following day. Investigation: In addition to your night action, you may choose a player and learn whether they are part of the Monster faction or not, if the Sheriff is dead, you may no longer perform this action.

Attack Types

During this game the following attacks exist: - Slay: A slain player will have their rolecard flipped and is removed from the game. - This can be done through special actions. - Banish to the End: A banished player will NOT have their rolecard flipped and is removed from the game. - This is done through the daily vote, the Monster Factional kill as well as special actions. - Missing: This is a state where a player is removed from the game and cannot be interacted with. It can occur in two different ways. - 1 night: If a player is only missing for a single night phase, this means that all visits on them fail that night, nothing more. - 1 day or multi phase: If a player is missing for a single day phase, or any amount of phases that exceeds a single night phase, the player is announced as missing. - Players in this state cannot interact with the game, as if dead, and cannot be interacted with. - If a player should return from this missing state, it will be announced.

Game Mechanics

Action Economy: Each phase, any player may perform ONE action they may have, unless an action states to be a BONUS action. Daily Vote: Each day, a vote is held in the Village, the player with the most votes is banished to the End! Monster Attack: Each night, the Monsters will invade the village, seeking to banish a player to the end in revenge! Multitasking: The Monsters may perform this action as a Bonus action. Monster Chat: The evil monsters share a chat together at all times.

Other Mechanics
