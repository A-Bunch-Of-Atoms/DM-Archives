In the village deep down, many people linger with their lives. They’re usually kept safe from the horrors of nature due to their terrain, but somehow, things have gotten dire. A flood has started in the ravine they reside in, and it’s brought in some unwelcome inhabitants. They lurk in the dark, their swimming unmatched by humanity. They move back and oversee the town, sending down one of their most zealous. A howl emanates, and the villagers scurry off into their homes...
— ” Hurry, hurry! We don’t have much time! “
A panicked whisper rises from the dark, a feminine figure running through the ravine with another more masculine one. They have but a small light in the way of a lantern to guide them; but they’ll make do. They quickly move into a crevice in the side, and then they let out laughs, their insatiable desire for trouble will be fulfilled here. The elders had told then plenty of times of the danger of the entities in the dark, but nobody but the wise men believed them. They thought them fairy-tales, but they were real, and they were ready for a victim; and now, two. With their laughing dialing down, a small growl comes from the other side of the crack. The woman struggles immediately to get out, while the man tries to move towards the noise, ready to fight. The woman hyperventilates and tries to get away to safety, but she’s struggling. Bleeding as she tries to hopelessly get free from the rocks she had trapped herself between. The man on the other hand had gone silent, with an undying will to live the girl finally escapes and runs for safety before being stopped in her track by a foul beast, a shadow in the moons light. The eyes spoke of something inhuman but once man, something that had lost its way long ago. She backs up slowly: she learned this from the elders, if you back up slowly from beasts they wouldn’t attack, but unfortunately wisdom only knew so much about nature. The beast flew forward in a lunge, desperate to grab the outsider.

” Shit, shit.. what’re we doing?! Where is he? Fuck! “
Speaking to herself, she tries to run the other way towards the edge of the ravine. Maybe she’d be able to put it off balance, but she finds herself unable to escape the beast; who quickly grabs onto her leg, claws digging deep. In one final movement, she moves forward in an attempt to kill the beast alongside herself. Whilst the beast struggles, it suddenly moves forward, pushing the girl back. The beast falls off of the edge whilst the girl coughs blood, attempting to stand. Her vision had gone a dark orange, something was terribly wrong. She looked at her arms, hair had begun to grow where it shouldn’t. Arms becoming full of hair and teeth becoming sharper than anything she’d ever seen in a tool, she begged for help. She begged for him. But he never answered, for he too found himself in a predicament unlike any other, cornered by the beasts. But these ones didn’t try to kill him, they simply cut deep into his arm. The beasts pushed him back into the crevice for him to escape, and as he does so, he runs into his partner once more. His vision had gone the same orange as the girl, and they both begin to feel a sense of unease, a heat in their body unable to be cleansed. They wished to be partners, and now they were. They’d be partners forever, and if either were to die, the other would quickly follow. They knew what they had to do as their minds began to transform into the beasts own. They had to bring others into this pack, they had to bring the village back to them, for they knew they wouldn’t be welcomed as they were.
With a simple thought, their hair began to recede and teeth returned to normal, wounds closing as if they were never there. Heading back to their houses, the howl of the wolves became nigh unbearable, tonight would be restless.
